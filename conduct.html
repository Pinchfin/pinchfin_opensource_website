Community Standards

Community standards for pinchfin.

pinchfin exists at the intersection of financial services and open source software. Both communities have high standards for rigor, transparency, and professional conduct. These standards apply to all contributors, users, and participants in the pinchfin ecosystem.

1. Be Constructive

Contributions should advance the quality and capability of the system. Criticism is welcome when it is specific, actionable, and directed at the work rather than the person. If you identify a problem, propose a solution or clearly describe what would constitute improvement.

Unconstructive commentary, personal attacks, and dismissive responses are not acceptable. The goal is structured collaboration, not competitive posturing.

2. Be Precise

This is a credit analysis system. Precision matters. When reporting issues, provide specific inputs, expected outputs, and actual outputs. When proposing changes, document the rationale, the expected impact, and any downstream effects.

Vague issue reports, undocumented code changes, and assumptions presented as facts undermine the system's reliability. Treat contributions with the same rigor you would apply to a credit memo.

3. Respect Expertise

Contributors come from diverse backgrounds: commercial real estate, software engineering, data science, financial services, and regulatory compliance. Each perspective is valuable. Respect domain expertise you do not possess. Ask questions before making assumptions about areas outside your specialization.

Do not assume that financial experience substitutes for engineering rigor, or that engineering capability substitutes for credit judgment. Both are required. Neither is sufficient alone.

4. Protect Confidentiality

Deal data, borrower information, property details, and financial records must never appear in public repositories, issue trackers, discussion forums, or any other public-facing medium. All examples in documentation and issue reports must use synthetic or anonymized data.

If you discover confidential information in any public-facing resource, report it immediately. Do not reproduce or redistribute it.

5. Acknowledge Limitations

This system has limitations. It makes mistakes. Its outputs require human review. Contributors should honestly represent what the system can and cannot do. Do not overstate capabilities in documentation, marketing materials, or community discussions.

When the system produces incorrect output, treat it as an opportunity for improvement rather than a failure to conceal. Transparency about limitations builds trust. Concealment destroys it.

Enforcement

Violations of these standards may result in removal of contributions, restriction of access to community resources, or permanent exclusion from the project. Enforcement decisions are made by the project maintainers and are final.

Reports of violations can be submitted through the project's GitHub issue tracker or by contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with appropriate confidentiality.

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: the GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any other community forums associated with pinchfin.
